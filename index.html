A Tribute to Angus

There is no "normal"
Chris Owen and Charlie Talbert in Angus (image provided by IMDb)

About

Angus was released in 1995 and tells the story of an awkward, overweight, and self-conscious teenager as he goes through a rough year in school. Over the course of the movie we see Angus and his friend bullied in school and deal with even harder issues out of school, such as the passing of his grandfather. Angus winds up getting elected as king to the Winter Ball as a joke by the main bully in the film, Rick. As expected, things continue poorly leading up to the dance but after a confrontation with Rick at the dance Angus makes an impactful speech to confront Rick and lays out that there is no “normal” as everyone is different. He wins over the rest of the class as well as his crush, who was the queen of the Winter Ball.

Impact

Although I did not know it at the time, Angus did not have a great reception on release. I'm also sure that many people who view it today would also not give it much praise. But at the time I first saw Angus I was 8 years old and held many of the same "abnormal" traits that Angus, the character, had. I also saw these same traits in other people in my life. I really connected with the character and credit the movie for helping me to become an empathetic individual. To this day I still tell myself there is no “normal” and I try to pass that same mindset on to others I meet.
